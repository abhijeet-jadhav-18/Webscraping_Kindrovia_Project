<h4 class="wp-block-heading">Our Assessment</h4>
<!-- /wp:heading -->
<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How School of American Ballet performs</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:columns -->
<div class="wp-block-columns">
<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column">
<!-- wp:heading {"level":5} --><h5 class="wp-block-heading">Expert Staff</h5><!-- /wp:heading -->
<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} --><h3 class="wp-block-heading">Coaching Credentials</h3><!-- /wp:heading -->
<!-- wp:paragraph --><p>The School of American Ballet’s training is designed for dedicated, focused students who show age-appropriate aptitude and physical development suited to the demands of classical training at all levels of development. The curriculum spans beginning instruction for 6 year olds through highly advanced courses designed to launch the nation’s most talented students into professional careers.</p><!-- /wp:paragraph -->
</div>
<!-- /wp:column -->
<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column">
<!-- wp:heading {"level":5} --><h5 class="wp-block-heading">Student Care</h5><!-- /wp:heading -->
<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} --><h3 class="wp-block-heading">Student Wellbeing</h3><!-- /wp:heading -->
<!-- wp:paragraph --><p>Although the emphasis at SAB is on training rather than performance, students have unique opportunities for stage experience through SAB’s programs and through its affiliation with New York City Ballet. Performances for Advanced Division students include the Spring Workshop, SAB’s annual fully staged production; the SAB Student Choreography Workshop; the New York Choreographic Institute, NYCB’s lecture-demonstration program and SAB’s Beauty of Ballet community outreach performances in Brooklyn, Queens, Harlem and Bronx.</p><!-- /wp:paragraph -->
</div>
<!-- /wp:column -->
<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column">
<!-- wp:heading {"level":5} --><h5 class="wp-block-heading">Curriculum</h5><!-- /wp:heading -->
<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} --><h3 class="wp-block-heading">Academic Integration</h3><!-- /wp:heading -->
<!-- wp:paragraph --><p>The Preparatory Division enrolls 6 and 7 year olds who are either new to ballet or who have taken creative movement and beginning ballet classes elsewhere. The Preparatory curriculum focuses on the basic elements of ballet technique and nurtures the discipline that focused ballet training demands.</p><!-- /wp:paragraph -->
</div>
<!-- /wp:column -->
</div>
<!-- /wp:columns -->

<!-- wp:columns -->
<div class="wp-block-columns">
<!-- wp:column -->
<div class="wp-block-column">
<!-- wp:heading {"level":5} --><h5 class="wp-block-heading">Achievements</h5><!-- /wp:heading -->
<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} --><h3 class="wp-block-heading">Competitive Pathway</h3><!-- /wp:heading -->
<!-- wp:paragraph --><p>We undertake the broadest possible recruiting efforts to select students ages 6 to 18 who possess the qualities necessary for the highest level of achievement as classical ballet dancers, with the ultimate mission of placing our graduates in dance companies around the globe. Our vision of excellence is underpinned by the cultivation of a diverse student population in which the unique gifts and perspectives of all students are embraced.</p><!-- /wp:paragraph -->
</div>
<!-- /wp:column -->

Typically, children enrolled in Girls II-V and Boys II-V are eligible for consideration.</p><!-- /wp:paragraph -->
